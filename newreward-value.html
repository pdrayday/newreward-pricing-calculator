The Landscape

Two battlegrounds. One system.

Classic search still drives demand — rankings, the map pack, reviews. But a growing share of buying decisions now starts as a question to an AI assistant, and those assistants don't show ten options. They recommend one or two. If your business isn't the answer, a competitor is — and you never see the customer you lost.

The two battlegrounds feed each other: AI engines learn who to trust largely from the same signals that drive search — your rankings, your reviews, what credible third parties say about you, and how clearly your site answers real questions. That's why we run SEO and AEO/GEO as one program, not two line items.

Behind the Scenes

The proof loop: diagnose, fix, prove. Every month.

Most agencies hand you a report. Most software hands you a score. We run a closed loop — and every cycle ends with evidence you can inspect, not a promise.

Find where you disappear

We test how you actually appear — and where you vanish — across search and every major AI engine, the way a real customer would ask.

  • Prompt-level visibility scans across your high-intent buyer searches
  • Citation, competitor, and trust-gap analysis
  • Entity, local, and on-site signal review — ranked by revenue impact
02 — FIX

Ship the work that moves it

Diagnosis is the easy part. Our team executes the fixes — across content, technical structure, reputation, and your conversion path.

  • Answer-ready content engineered for how AI reads, cites, and recommends
  • Schema, entity, and trust-signal work AI systems verify against
  • Business profile, reviews, and location signals that local answers depend on
03 — PROVE

Show what changed

We re-scan the same surfaces after the work ships. You see movement — or you see exactly what's still open and what we're doing about it.

  • Before/after re-scans on the surfaces buyers actually use
  • Plain-language reporting tied to demand, not vanity charts
  • Senior guidance on what to fix next — and what to skip
Why this is hard to do in-house: each AI engine weighs trust differently, the rules shift constantly, and the work spans five different crafts — analysis, content, technical, reputation, and reporting. We run this loop across clients every day with a dedicated six-person team of senior specialists. For one business to replicate it means hiring or becoming all five — and staying current in a field that changes monthly. The question isn't whether you could learn pieces of it. It's whether that's the best use of the hours that run your business.
